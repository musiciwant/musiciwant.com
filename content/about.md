## Why This Exists

Some people can't just press play. A sudden cymbal crash. An unexpected scream in a chorus. A bass drop that wasn't coming a second ago. For people with sensory processing sensitivities — autism, ADHD, anxiety, SPD, or simply being a highly sensitive person — these moments aren't just startling. They hurt.

And yet, there's no way to know what a song will do to you before you hear it.

Music I Want changes that. Every song in our library is rated for sensory sensitivity — dynamic range, sudden changes, texture, predictability. So you can find music that fits how you experience sound, and listen with confidence instead of fear.

## How the Ratings Work

Every song in the library has a sensory profile:

- **Dynamic Range** (1-10) — How much the volume varies. 1 is very consistent. 10 is extreme swings.
- **Sudden Changes** — None, mild, moderate, frequent, or extreme. Does the song surprise you?
- **Texture** — Smooth, layered, complex, harsh, or abrasive. How the sound feels.
- **Predictability** — High, medium, or low. Can you trust what's coming next?
- **Vocal Style** — Instrumental, soft vocals, spoken word, dynamic vocals, or screaming.
- **Sensory Notes** — Specific warnings or reassurances about moments in the song.
- **Recommended For** — What this song is good for: sleep, focus, anxiety relief, meltdown recovery.
